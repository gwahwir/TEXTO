Title: Social Safety Net and Economic Security

Social security reform has become increasingly urgent as demographic shifts — a growing retired population supported by a proportionally smaller working-age cohort — threaten the long-term solvency of the programme. The range of potential adjustments is well-understood: benefit modifications, revenue increases, or some combination. The political will to act ahead of a genuine crisis, however, has been consistently lacking.

Medicare expansion has been proposed in various forms, from adding dental care access, vision care coverage, and hearing aid affordability to the existing benefit structure, to more ambitious proposals for Medicare-for-All or a public option. Each expansion is evaluated against its cost, its effect on private insurance markets, and its feasibility as a legislative matter.

SNAP benefits expansion has significant anti-poverty effects. The evidence on food stamp drug testing — which several states have implemented — suggests that it is expensive to administer, reaches very few people with substance use disorders, and is more effective as a political signal than as a policy intervention.

Universal basic income pilots have been conducted in a number of cities, providing direct cash payments to low-income residents. The results have generally shown positive effects on employment, food security, and mental health, challenging assumptions that unconditional cash transfers reduce work incentives.

Earned income tax credit expansion remains one of the most evidence-backed anti-poverty interventions available, particularly for working families with children. The child tax credit expansion of 2021 demonstrated that near-universal child allowances can dramatically reduce child poverty in a short period.

Benefits cliffs and marginal tax rates created by means-tested programs can create powerful disincentives to increased earnings at income levels where multiple benefits phase out simultaneously. Simplification and better coordination across programmes could significantly improve work incentives.

Disability rights and access encompasses both anti-discrimination law and the range of supports that enable people with disabilities to participate fully in economic and social life. Disability employment incentives have shown promise in increasing labour force participation.

Child welfare and foster care reform has moved toward family preservation models that support families at risk of separation rather than defaulting to removal of children.
